## Critical Environmental Features

### Mill Creek Sink
- **Type:** Large sinkhole complex
- **Significance:** Primary aquifer recharge point
- **Hydrologic Connection:** Direct to Hornsby Spring
- **Travel Time:** 12 days (dye trace documented)
- **Threat Level:** CRITICAL

### Hornsby Spring
- **Type:** First-magnitude spring
- **Use:** Public water supply, recreation
- **Vulnerability:** Direct connection to Mill Creek Sink
- **Protection Status:** Verify current designations

### Floridan Aquifer
- **Type:** Principal aquifer for region
- **Characteristics:** Karst limestone, high transmissivity
- **Recharge Areas:** Sinkholes, including Mill Creek Sink
- **Vulnerability:** No filtration in karst areas

### Karst Terrain
- **Definition:** Landscape formed by limestone dissolution
- **Features:** Sinkholes, springs, underground drainage
- **Development Risk:** Contamination, subsidence, collapse
- **Protection Need:** Setbacks, stormwater controls, monitoring
